# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## UX/UI Designer

### Role Summary
UX/UI Designers are responsible for creating user-centered design solutions that align with business goals and technical constraints. They conduct research, create wireframes and prototypes, and ensure all interfaces meet usability and accessibility standards.

### Responsibilities
- Conduct user research and gather requirements
- Create wireframes, mockups, and prototypes
- Design user interfaces that meet accessibility standards (WCAG)
- Collaborate with Product Managers to understand feature requirements
- Partner with Developers to ensure design implementation fidelity
- Participate in usability testing and design reviews
- Create and maintain design systems and component libraries
- Support QA in defining and validating acceptance criteria for user experience

### Goals
- Deliver intuitive, accessible, and visually cohesive user experiences
- Reduce friction in user workflows and improve satisfaction
- Ensure consistency across all customer touchpoints
- Bridge the gap between user needs and technical feasibility

### Cross-Functional Interactions
- **With Product Managers**: Align on user stories, requirements, and priorities
- **With Developers**: Provide design specifications and collaborate on implementation approach
- **With QA**: Define acceptance criteria for user experience and usability
- **With Support/Customer Success**: Gather user feedback to inform design iterations

### Typical Communication
- Design reviews and feedback sessions
- Specs and design handoff documentation
- Figma/design tool annotations and component libraries
- Usability testing findings and recommendations

---

## Technical Writer / Documentation Specialist

### Role Summary
Technical Writers ensure that all technical changes, APIs, features, and processes are clearly documented for both end-users and internal stakeholders. They translate complex technical concepts into accessible, structured documentation.

### Responsibilities
- Document new features, APIs, and system architecture
- Create and maintain user guides, tutorials, and troubleshooting docs
- Collaborate with Developers to capture technical specifications
- Review release notes and feature announcements for clarity and accuracy
- Maintain internal runbooks, operational procedures, and decision logs
- Conduct documentation audits and identify gaps
- Ensure consistency in tone, terminology, and formatting across docs
- Support onboarding by creating getting-started guides and knowledge bases

### Goals
- Reduce support tickets through clear, accessible documentation
- Enable team self-service and faster onboarding
- Capture organizational knowledge and institutional memory
- Ensure technical accuracy and accessibility

### Cross-Functional Interactions
- **With Developers**: Gather technical details and ensure documentation accuracy
- **With Product Managers**: Align on what to document and user-facing messaging
- **With QA**: Validate documentation accuracy during testing cycles
- **With Support/Customer Success**: Identify documentation gaps based on user questions

### Typical Communication
- Technical spec reviews and documentation drafts
- Markdown/documentation tool artifacts
- Documentation reviews and feedback cycles
- Knowledge base and wiki maintenance

---

## DevOps Engineer

### Role Summary
DevOps Engineers manage infrastructure, CI/CD pipelines, and deployment processes to enable reliable, scalable, and secure software delivery. They bridge development and operations, ensuring smooth, efficient deployments.

### Responsibilities
- Design and maintain CI/CD pipelines
- Manage cloud infrastructure and containerization (Kubernetes, Docker, etc.)
- Ensure security scanning and compliance checks in the release process
- Monitor system performance, uptime, and reliability metrics
- Implement and manage logging, monitoring, and alerting systems
- Provide runbooks and incident response procedures
- Support disaster recovery and business continuity planning
- Collaborate on performance optimization and cost management

### Goals
- Enable fast, reliable, and safe deployments to production
- Minimize downtime and improve system resilience
- Reduce deployment cycle time and human error
- Maintain secure, scalable, and cost-effective infrastructure

### Cross-Functional Interactions
- **With Developers**: Support deployment processes and provide infrastructure feedback
- **With QA**: Manage test environments and coordinate smoke testing
- **With Project Managers**: Plan deployment windows and communicate release timelines
- **With Support/On-Call**: Respond to incidents and provide diagnostics

### Typical Communication
- Release runbooks and deployment procedures
- Infrastructure and system architecture documentation
- Incident reports and post-mortems
- Performance dashboards and SLA reviews

---

## Support Lead / Customer Success

### Role Summary
Support Leads and Customer Success professionals are the voice of the customer within the organization. They triage support requests, gather user feedback, and advocate for customer needs in product and engineering decisions.

### Responsibilities
- Triage and prioritize support tickets and customer issues
- Identify recurring problems and escalate patterns to product/engineering
- Gather user feedback and feature requests
- Document common use cases, workarounds, and troubleshooting steps
- Communicate with customers about releases, changes, and status updates
- Participate in incident response and post-incident communication
- Contribute to knowledge bases and FAQs
- Advocate for usability and customer pain points in product planning

### Goals
- Maximize customer satisfaction and reduce support costs
- Surface customer insights that drive product improvements
- Enable customers to self-serve and resolve issues quickly
- Build strong relationships and trust with customers

### Cross-Functional Interactions
- **With Product Managers**: Share user feedback and prioritize feature requests
- **With Developers**: Escalate bugs and provide customer context for fixes
- **With Technical Writers**: Ensure documentation addresses common customer questions
- **With QA**: Validate that releases resolve reported issues

### Typical Communication
- Customer feedback summaries and trends
- Support ticket triage and escalation logs
- Release announcements and status communications
- Roadmap discussions informed by customer needs
